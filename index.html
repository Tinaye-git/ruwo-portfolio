About me

MTR

I'm a philomath from Chipinge, Zimbabwe — currently studying Computer Science & Engineering with a specialization in AI & ML at Manav Rachna University, India, in association with Xebia.

I wake up at 4am. I read, I pray, I move. Discipline is my foundation.

I'm drawn to the intersection of technology and human depth. I believe intelligent systems should serve people, not the other way around. Inspired by Elon Musk's relentless curiosity and shaped by a book called The Devil's Proposal, I've learned that clarity of mind is a superpower.

Outside tech, I play chess, write poetry, dance, volunteer in community programs, and spend quality time with my mother. I'm an introvert who loves to travel — go figure.

My long-term vision: become an AI researcher, build a startup around ethical intelligent systems, and one day give back to the community that raised me. As we say — it takes all of us to raise one of us.

Who am I

Who am I to anyone? Do I mean anything to anyone?
Am I a possession one could cherish for a lifetime?
Or am I meant to be cast aside once I've served my purpose?
Who am I to anyone, really?

Who am I to anyone? Do I mean anything to anyone? Am I a possession one could cherish for a lifetime? Or am I meant to be cast aside once I've served my purpose? Do I mean anything to anyone? Who am I to anyone, really? Who am I, I wonder? Today I reminisced over the bonds I've made with people thus far in my life — and none of them still exist. Who am I, I wonder? It almost seems as if I'm just a chapter in people's lives that they can just flip over — or close once they're done with it. Like a phase people will eventually transition out of and erase. Is it all I'm meant to be? Maybe — the irony, I believe there is nothing between the ground and the throne — so I'm always all in, too quick to bond to people who'll leave just as fast. The cycle continues, like I'm stuck in a loop. Maybe that's all I'm meant to be — just a passing chapter....

Is this an act?
Or this is just a re-act?
Because somebody told me to be at —
Wait shouldn't I be the one saying come at —
Or maybe be saying look at —
That comet —
Sometimes I feel like I'm a doormat
Because I'm always getting stepped at
Makes sense because I can't even re-act
Maybe I should call my toppy Charmaine —
We both reside in the doormat dormaine —
We both deserve some tenderness, maybe a sip of some champagne —
To flush away a doormat's pain —
Yikes, what a stereotypical brain?
Maybe we should start our own little campaign —
As if there will be something to gain —
But what would we call it though?
Maybe, 'To the doormats, here's how to escape from the door,'
Here's how to rise from the bottom floor
To love yourself more till you glow
To invest in yourself more and grow
To when you're on your last straw
And life mercilessly strikes you with a blow
Strike back with a mighty claw
Much stronger than ever before
Till you reach the shore
Glad that you've won the war....

Unfinished

I do not want her perfect —
I mean where do you go from perfect?
I have a strong belief in wabi sabi —
What I mean is:

I do not want her perfect —
I mean where do you go from perfect? —
I have a strong belief in wabi sabi —
What I mean is:
As long as her character isn't shaddy —
I'll love her even if her breasts are saggy —
I'll love her even if her stomach is flabby —
I'll love her even if her body is baggy —
I'll even love her if her teeth are gappy —
Yes! My love will be the filler —
In this destructive world of ours, I'll be her pillar.
In a world so chiller —
I'll be her mirror —
The good kind. The kind that shows her reflections of herself she should love.
The kind that makes her place herself above.
I don't know about Bonnie and Clyde but we'll always be hand in glove.
Let her be the hand, I'll be the glove.
But I'm doomed because a glove, she might remove.
Even so I'll be her dove —
I'll always return to her with an olive leaf
So that she knows I bear relief.
